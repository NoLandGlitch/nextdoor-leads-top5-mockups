## Trend notes informing the redesigns
Use these as the website strategy layer, not as visible citations.

### Veterinary / animal care trends (2025 behavior direction)
From reviewed sources and summaries:
- pet owners expect easier digital communication, online scheduling, access to records, reminders, and less phone friction
- clinics benefit from strong booking flows, forms, and follow-up communication
- educational content still matters, but should be easier to navigate

### Fitness trends (2025)
- personalization is king
- hybrid fitness and flexible scheduling matter
- recovery is now mainstream
- community and inclusivity are major conversion drivers
- longevity / sustainable wellness positioning works better than pure aesthetics

### Restaurant trends (2025–2026 website direction)
- mobile speed and direct ordering matter most
- visible CTA above the fold is critical
- real food photography improves conversion
- loyalty / specials / catering and clear menus help conversion
- guests want authenticity and memorable but simple experiences

### Bar / sports bar trends (2025)
- consumers spend more carefully and respond to authentic local experiences
- tech should remove friction, not add gimmicks
- events, specials, drinks, and memorable moments should be immediately visible
- menu, call-to-order, hours, and location should be easy on mobile

## Functional requirements
- Use semantic HTML.
- No build step. Plain HTML/CSS/JS only.
- Navigation should work locally by opening `index.html` in a browser.
- Each business page should have:
  - hero with logo
  - strong CTA area
  - services/features/menu/membership/event sections based on the real business
  - trust section / testimonials style block / differentiators
  - contact + hours section
  - clear mobile-friendly layout
- The showcase homepage should present all five businesses as polished concept cards linking to their pages.
- Add a tasteful note in the README that these are concept redesigns built from public information and public brand assets.

## Quality bar
- This should feel like a portfolio-quality outreach deliverable.
- Avoid generic lorem ipsum.
- Copy should stay grounded in real public business info above.
- Do not invent fake awards, fake reviews, or fake doctors/trainers/staff names.
- You can use realistic placeholder CTA labels like “Book Appointment”, “View Memberships”, “Order Online”, “See Specials”, etc.
- Ensure consistent responsive design.
- Make the pages visually distinct while still feeling like one cohesive project.
